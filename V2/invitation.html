19th Annual Durga Puja 2017, Helsinki


Dear friends,

The arrival of autumn tells us that Durga Puja will soon be on its way here and brings to mind the cherished sounds of the dhak and conch. We are pleased to invite you to this auspicious occasion, which marks the victory of Good over Evil. This year we, the Bengalis in Finland, will be celebrating this festival of joy with our family and friends from 29th September to 1st October 2017. In this event, you will be able to experience our rich culture and customs, as colourful as the Finnish autumn.

The program for the three days is as follows:

29th September 17.00-22.00 Decorations, Puja & Dinner 30th September 09.00-14.00 Puja, Anjali, Prasad & Lunch 15.30-16.30 Children’s Sit & Draw 17.00-17.45 Puja, Aarati & Anjali 18.00-20.00 Cultural Program 20.00-21.00 Dinner 1st October 09.00-14.00 Puja, Anjali, Prasad & Lunch 14.00-18.00 Children's Sit & Draw and games, Cultural program & other activities 18.00-20.00 Puja, Aarati, Bishorjon, Shidur Khela & Dinner - Children’s Sit & Draw: Participants need to bring own coloring box, drawing papers will be provided. Register your child by sending name and age by email with subject "Children's Sit & Draw 2017". Venue: Asukastalo Kulkuri, Rastilanpolku 1, 00980 Helsinki (near Rastila metro station)

Registration is compulsory - please confirm your participation by Saturday, 22nd September 2017 to finben@finben.org along with payment confirmation. Due to limited space we can only accept the registration of 100 guests on a first-come, first-served basis.

Registration fee:

Season Pass for 3 days: 40€/Adult & 15€/Child (5-12 yrs)
Day Pass for one full day only: 20€/Adult & 5€/Child (5-12 yrs)
Cultural Evening Pass (Saturday): 15€/Adult & 5€/Child (5-12 yrs)
Children below 5 yrs are free. Students who don't receive any KELA benefits will be able to get a discounted season pass for 20€. No discounts available for other passes.

Also remember to express your creativity by submitting a work for the Pujo magazine Srijan, for more details check out our Facebook page.

NOTICE OF FILMING AT PUJA PREMISES

We would like to inform you that during this year’s Durga Puja there will be occasional filming in the main puja hall during all three days. Since the filming involves 360° technology, everyone present in the scene will be recorded on film. This is being done for a virtual reality project on Durga Puja celebrations in Helsinki, which will later be available in the public domain. When coming to the puja venue, please make you sure you sign your approval to be filmed (and for your children). If you would prefer not to be filmed, inform us all the same, in which case you will have to temporarily stay outside the filming zone.
